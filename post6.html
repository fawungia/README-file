What Order to Apply Skincare Products (Morning vs Night).

By Sarah • June 18, 2026 6 Min Read

One of the most common questions I get asked is: "I bought all these great products, but how on earth do I layer them?" You could buy the most effective serum in the world, but if you apply it over a thick oil, it’s never going to penetrate.

The golden rule of skincare application is simple: Apply from thinnest to thickest consistency.

The Morning Routine: Protect and Defend

Mornings are all about protecting your skin from the day ahead (sun, pollution, stress).

  1. Cleanse: A splash of water or a very gentle cleanser.
  2. Toner/Essence: If you use a watery toner, pat it in now.
  3. Antioxidant Serum: Usually your Vitamin C. Apply to dry or slightly damp skin.
  4. Hydrating Serum: Use the Daily Reset Serum to lock in moisture.
  5. Moisturizer: A light lotion to seal everything in.
  6. Sunscreen: The non-negotiable final step.

The Evening Routine: Cleanse and Repair

Nights are for undoing the day's damage and repair.

  1. Double Cleanse: Start with an oil, followed by a water-based wash.
  2. Treatment: Apply your AHAs, BHAs, or retinoids.
  3. Hydrating Serum: Bring water back into the skin.
  4. Moisturizer: A thicker cream with ceramides to rebuild your barrier.
  5. Facial Oil: Oils are occlusive and should always be your absolute last step.
