Skin Barrier 101

By Editor • June 19 5 Min Read

The STRATUM CORNEUM—the outermost layer of your skin—is your body's first line of defense. When it's healthy, your skin is plump, glowing. When it's broken, everything else goes wrong.

Signs of a Broken Barrier

If your skin feels tight after washing or stings when you apply moisturizer, your barrier is likely compromised. This allows moisture to leak out and irritants to get in.

The 3-Step Repair Protocol

  • Strip it back: Stop all acids and retinoids immediately.
  • Replenish Lipids: Use products rich in Ceramides and Cholesterol.
  • Seal it in: Use an occlusive layer at night to prevent moisture loss.

Be patient. A barrier takes about 28 days to fully cycle and repair.
