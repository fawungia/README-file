My Evening Wind-Down Routine

How I take the day off and prep my skin for overnight recovery.

By the time 8 PM rolls around, my brain is usually fried from staring at screens all day, and my skin feels completely suffocated. Between the commute, the office AC, and the stress of balancing a busy schedule, my evening skincare routine has become less about "fixing" my face and more about preserving my sanity.

I don't do a 10-step routine. I don't have the energy for it, and honestly, neither does my skin barrier. My goal at night is simple: take the day off, release the physical tension in my face, and flood my skin with hydration so it can repair itself while I sleep.

Here is my realistic, deeply comforting evening wind-down routine.

Step 1: The "Melt" (Double Cleanse Part 1)

If you wear SPF or makeup, a water-based cleanser alone isn't going to cut it. I start with a soothing cleansing balm. I warm a scoop between my dry fingers and massage it into my dry skin for a full minute. This breaks down everything—even stubborn mascara—without me having to aggressively scrub my delicate eye area. Then, I emulsify it with a little warm water and rinse.

Step 2: The Clean Slate (Double Cleanse Part 2)

Next, I go in with a gentle, non-foaming hydrating cleanser. This removes the oily residue from the balm and cleans the actual skin underneath. Your skin should never feel "squeaky clean" or tight after this step. If it does, your cleanser is too harsh!

Step 3: The Prep & Treat

While my skin is still slightly damp, I pat in a hydrating serum (usually something heavy in Glycerin or Hyaluronic Acid). Applying it to damp skin acts like a sponge, pulling that moisture deep into the epidermis. If it’s an exfoliation night (only 2x a week!), this is when I’ll apply my lactic acid treatment instead to gently dissolve dead skin cells.

Step 4: The Tension Release (My Favorite Step)

This is my non-negotiable. I hold so much stress in my jaw and forehead by the end of the day. I grab the SkinHonestly Face Roller (I keep mine in the fridge for an extra cooling effect) and spend three minutes rolling upward and outward. The heavy, cold stainless steel feels incredible. It instantly depuffs, promotes lymphatic drainage, and physically relaxes my facial muscles so I don't clench my jaw as much when I sleep.

Step 5: Lock It All In

Finally, I seal all that hydration and hard work in with a thick, barrier-repairing night cream. I look for ingredients like ceramides and peptides. I take whatever is left on my fingers and rub it into my neck and the backs of my hands.

The Takeaway

That’s it. It takes about 10 minutes from start to finish, but those 10 minutes are the exact boundary my brain needs to switch off from "work mode" and transition into "rest mode."
