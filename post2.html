My Evening Wind-Down Routine for Recovery.

By Editor • July 01, 2026 5 Min Read

By the time 8 PM rolls around, my brain is usually fried from staring at screens all day, and my skin feels completely suffocated. Between the commute, the office AC, and the stress of balancing a busy schedule, my evening skincare routine has become less about "fixing" my face and more about preserving my sanity.

I don't do a 10-step routine. I don't have the energy for it, and honestly, neither does my skin barrier. My goal at night is simple: take the day off, release the physical tension in my face, and flood my skin with hydration so it can repair itself while I sleep.

Step 1: The "Melt" (Double Cleanse Part 1)

If you wear SPF or makeup, a water-based cleanser alone isn't going to cut it. I start with a soothing cleansing balm. I massage it into my dry skin for a full minute to break down stubborn mascara without scrubbing.

Step 2: The Clean Slate (Double Cleanse Part 2)

Next, I go in with a gentle, non-foaming hydrating cleanser. Your skin should never feel "squeaky clean" or tight after this step. If your cleanser makes your face feel like a desert, it's too harsh!

Step 3: The Prep & Treat

While my skin is still slightly damp, I pat in a hydrating serum heavy in Glycerin or Hyaluronic Acid. Applying it to damp skin pulls that moisture deep into the epidermis.

Finally, I seal everything in with a rich, ceramide-heavy night cream. Wake up with skin that feels rested, even if your brain doesn't.
