Peptides Explained

By Editor • June 21 5 Min Read

Think of your skin as a brick wall. Collagen and elastin are the mortar holding everything together. As we age, that mortar starts to break down. Peptides are tiny fragments of proteins that send signals to your cells, "tricking" them into producing more collagen.

How They Work

Peptides act as messengers. When applied topically, they signal the skin to perform specific functions like building collagen, soothing inflammation, or increasing hydration.

  • Signal Peptides: These tell your skin to make more collagen.
  • Carrier Peptides: These deliver trace minerals to the skin.
  • Enzyme-Inhibitor Peptides: These slow down the natural breakdown of collagen.

For those looking for long-term remodeling and firmness without irritation, Peptides are an essential tool.
