If there is one question that pops up in my DMs more than any other, it’s this: "Do I actually need to buy a separate eye cream?"

The skin around your eyes is indeed thinner, more delicate, and lacks the oil glands found on the rest of your face. Because of this, it gets drier faster and is usually the first place to show fine lines. However, a solid, fragrance-free facial moisturizer is perfectly capable of keeping your under-eyes hydrated and protected.

When Should You Buy an Eye Cream?

You only need a dedicated eye cream if you are trying to treat a specific under-eye issue that your regular moisturizer can’t handle:

  • Dark Circles & Puffiness: If you wake up with puffy eyes, a standard face lotion won't help. You need an eye cream formulated with caffeine to constrict blood vessels and reduce swelling. (I've linked an amazing, budget-friendly caffeine eye serum in the Shop that works like a double-shot of espresso for your face).
  • Deep Crow's Feet: If you want to tackle wrinkles, you need retinol. However, normal face retinoids are often too strong for the delicate eye area. A retinol eye cream is specifically formulated at a lower concentration to prevent severe irritation.

The Bottom Line

If your current routine is keeping your under-eyes soft and happy, save your money. Just bring your regular hydrating serums and gentle moisturizers up to the eye area. But if you're battling specific concerns like persistent puffiness or fine lines, investing in a targeted treatment might finally give you the results you're looking for.

Skincare is personal, and there are no absolute rules—only what works best for your skin and your budget!
